# Adr-classification-
Case assessment and classification 
Case 1
Patient: 35-year-old male
Drug: Amoxicillin
ADR: Nausea and vomiting
Severity: Mild
Case Classification: Probable
Explanation: Nausea and vomiting are common, non-serious adverse effects of amoxicillin. The reaction is likely related to the drug and improves after discontinuation or treatment.
Case 2
Patient: 42-year-old female
Drug: Ciprofloxacin
ADR: Skin rash and itching
Severity: Moderate
Case Classification: Probable
Explanation: Skin rash is a known adverse effect of ciprofloxacin. The reaction causes discomfort and may require treatment but is not life-threatening.
Case 3
Patient: 28-year-old female
Drug: Carbamazepine
ADR: Stevens-Johnson Syndrome (SJS)
Severity: Severe
Case Classification: Certain
Explanation: SJS is a serious and potentially life-threatening skin reaction strongly associated with carbamazepine. The causal relationship is well established.
Case 4
Patient: 60-year-old male
Drug: Human Insulin
ADR: Severe hypoglycemia requiring hospitalization
Severity: Serious
Case Classification: Probable
Explanation: Insulin can cause hypoglycemia if the dose is excessive or food intake is inadequate. Hospitalization makes this a serious adverse event.
Case 5
Patient: 50-year-old female
Drug: Penicillin Injection
ADR: Anaphylactic shock
Severity: Fatal
Case Classification: Certain
Explanation: Anaphylactic shock is a severe allergic reaction that can occur immediately after penicillin administration and may result in death if not treated promptly
